What Goes Where? Budget Category Examples

üè† Needs (50%) - Essential Expenses

  • Housing: Rent/mortgage, property taxes, home insurance
  • Utilities: Electricity, water, gas, internet, phone
  • Food: Groceries (not dining out)
  • Transportation: Car payment, gas, insurance, public transit
  • Healthcare: Health insurance, prescriptions, doctor visits
  • Minimum Debt Payments: Minimum on credit cards, student loans
  • Childcare: Daycare, after-school care (if working parents)

üéâ Wants (30%) - Discretionary Spending

  • Dining & Entertainment: Restaurants, bars, coffee shops, takeout
  • Subscriptions: Netflix, Spotify, gym memberships, magazines
  • Shopping: Clothes, electronics, home decor (non-essential)
  • Hobbies: Sports, crafts, gaming, books
  • Travel: Vacations, weekend trips
  • Personal Care: Salon, spa, cosmetics beyond basics
  • Gifts: Birthday, holiday, wedding gifts

üí∞ Savings & Debt (20%) - Building Wealth

  • Emergency Fund: Save 3-6 months of expenses
  • Retirement: 401(k), IRA, Roth IRA contributions
  • Debt Payoff: Extra payments above minimums on loans
  • Investments: Stocks, bonds, index funds
  • Savings Goals: Down payment, education, big purchases
  • Additional Insurance: Life, disability income protection

üí° Tips for Sticking to Your Budget

  • Track your spending: Use apps like Mint or YNAB to monitor where money goes
  • Adjust if needed: If needs are over 50%, look for ways to reduce housing or transportation costs
  • Automate savings: Set up automatic transfers to savings on payday
  • Review monthly: Check if you're staying within categories and adjust
  • Use cash envelopes: For wants category, use cash to limit overspending
  • Prioritize debt with high interest: If you have credit card debt, focus 20% there first
